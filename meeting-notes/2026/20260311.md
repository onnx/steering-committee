## ONNX Model Zoo:
* They also addressed the ONNX Model Zoo repository, which Andreas noted is deprecated due to maintenance issues and security concerns.
* Sunny inquired about updating the offset for ONNX models, and Andreas confirmed this was discussed in an application note.
* The team agreed to consult with Javier Martinez and Daniel regarding the Model Zoo's future.
* LLM Distribution Strategy Discussion: The team discussed the distribution of modern LLM and GenAI models, particularly focusing on ONNX and Hugging Face platforms.
* Sunny emphasized the importance of making these models freely available and distributable, including IBM's proprietary Granite family of models, while highlighting the challenges of converting models from PyTorch to ONNX.
* Ganesan and Andreas noted the need to update tooling for Hugging Face integration and discussed the potential for collaboration between IBM and Microsoft on this initiative.
* The group agreed that hosting models on Hugging Face would be more practical than creating a separate ONNX model zoo, as long as the models remain accessible to the ONNX community.
* The team discussed the value and challenges of having a separate ONNX Models repository, with Javier noting that most GenAI models are already ported to Hugging Face and questioning the need for additional repositories.
* Sunny highlighted that not all GenAI models support ONNX operations and lack validation, while Ganesan explained that modern GenAI models often require custom ops for performance, though standardization efforts are ongoing.
* The group agreed that while more work is needed on standardized representations and custom ops, there is potential for common infrastructure to support model generation and validation across different architectures.
* The team discussed the importance of supporting a broader range of models in the ONNX ecosystem to keep it vibrant and accessible across different architectures.
* Sunny emphasized the need for a curated list of Gen AI models, while Ganesan highlighted ongoing efforts to improve model exportability and the exploration of a model builder approach.
* The group agreed on the value of having immediate ONNX versions of popular models published in Hugging Face, with Ganesan committing to follow up in a few weeks and Sunny offering to collaborate with Xavier and Rama on related work items.
